Wizard Game

Unity · C# · Multiplayer · 2025 – Present · Team project

What it is

A 4-player couch party game inspired by Kirby Air Riders. Phase 1 is a draft — each player collects stats and picks up to 2 spells from the arena. Phase 2 opens a vote: players choose which minigame to play next. The twist is that your loadout from phase 1 carries into whatever you voted for.

What I built

I owned the full UI layer — main menus, in-game HUD, the ready-up screen, and the voting interface. I also built the multiplayer setup and split-screen logic.

The part I'm most proud of: the dynamic split-screen camera. Rather than locking to fixed quadrants, the camera tracks only the players who are still alive, zooming out when they spread apart and zooming in when they cluster — all handled automatically at runtime. Getting the transitions to feel smooth rather than jarring took a lot of iteration.

What I learned

Local multiplayer has a surprising amount of edge cases. The ready-up system alone needed to handle players joining and leaving mid-lobby cleanly. I got a lot more comfortable with Unity's input system and event-driven architecture through this project.
